        when subject, section, and teacher records must be manually verified across
        disconnected references before release.

    ➢ How can secure student and parent portals be developed to provide authorized
        visibility to schedules, grades, and billing information?

        According to stakeholder-facing interview evidence in the appendices, delayed
        record synchronization affects how quickly families receive complete updates. The
        Finance interview (Appendix B.3) indicates that parent balance communication is
        not always immediate, and related field accounts show that guardians often rely on
        follow-ups when records are still being reconciled across offices.

    ➢ How can role dashboards and governance controls be developed to produce
        actionable indicators for enrollment trends, payment behavior, and operational
        risk monitoring?

        According to the Academic Head interview (Appendix B.1), leadership reporting
        is slowed when department outputs come from separate files and require manual
        consolidation before review. This confirms that management-level indicators for
        enrollment movement, payment status, and academic monitoring are often delayed
        by fragmented data preparation practices.
