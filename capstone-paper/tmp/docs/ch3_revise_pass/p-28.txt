The model applies the following stages to maintain user-centered and operationally
aligned development:

    1. 1. Requirements Gathering and Analysis

        Interviews, process mapping, and role-task validation are used to identify
        bottlenecks in enrollment, cashiering, scheduling, grading, and reporting
        workflows.

    2. 2. Quick Design

        Initial page flows, data mappings, and module interaction logic are prepared based
        on verified responsibilities and system boundaries.

    3. 3. Build Prototype

        A working prototype is produced for core operational modules, including registrar
        intake, finance posting, instructional grading, and role dashboards.

    4. 4. Initial User Evaluation

        Key users from registrar, finance, academic, and administration evaluate usability,
        output validity, and workflow fit against actual office practice.

    5. 5. Refine Prototype

        Evaluation feedback is applied iteratively to improve data accuracy, process
        speed, and interface clarity until acceptance criteria are satisfied.

    6. 6. Implement and Maintain

        Validated modules are rolled out for controlled use, followed by user orientation,
        policy alignment, and maintenance planning for sustained operations.
