Therefore, the project context must be interpreted as a systems-level coordination problem
with      compliance,    governance,   and   stakeholder-service   dimensions    operating
simultaneously. MarriottConnect is positioned to solve this by unifying module workflows,
preserving role-based access discipline, and transforming validated operations into
analytics-ready evidence for planning. In this framework, the problem statements below
are not abstract design questions; they are direct responses to documented process friction
and evidence-backed institutional needs observed in Marriott School and reinforced by
recent literature.




Statement of the Problem

This study defines the operational problems of Marriott School based on interviews with
key school stakeholders in the appendices, particularly the Academic Head (Appendix
B.1), Registrar (Appendix B.2), Finance Officer (Appendix B.3), and Teacher (Appendix
B.4). Their accounts consistently describe fragmented records, repeated reconciliation,
delayed cross-office validation, and limited management visibility during high-volume
cycles.

General Problem:

How can MarriottConnect be developed as an integrated student information system with
decision support analytics for Marriott School?

According to the Academic Head interview (Appendix B.1), departments still maintain
separate files and manually reconcile figures before reports can be finalized. According to
the Registrar interview (Appendix B.2), enrollment updates are still processed one-by-one
with repeated cross-checking across records. According to the Finance interview
(Appendix B.3), payment posting and balance confirmation can be delayed when
transactions occur in close succession.
