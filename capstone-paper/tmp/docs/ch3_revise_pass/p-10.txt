Governance and access control requirements further shape the project context. Schools
need role boundaries that prevent unauthorized edits while still allowing authorized users
to access what they need on time. According to Kanona (2022), central database controls
and role-scoped access are key to protecting information integrity in academic systems.
The same governance logic is reflected in MarriottConnect through policy-driven access
controls, audit visibility, and announcement/notification traceability, so transparency is
achieved without sacrificing operational discipline.

Cross-role communication is another decisive factor in institutional performance. When
offices and stakeholders rely on ad hoc messaging to confirm record status, coordination
overhead grows and official channels lose authority. According to Chai and Mostafa
(2021), integrated web-based school information environments improve communication
consistency by keeping users connected to one authoritative dataset. This is why
MarriottConnect pairs transaction modules with structured announcement and notification
workflows: operational decisions and stakeholder communication should reference the
same validated institutional data context.

At the leadership tier, the school's need is not only to store records but to interpret
operational movement in time for planning. Enrollment growth, payment behavior, grade-
submission cadence, and risk indicators all require timely synthesis to support proactive
decisions. According to Esquivel and Esquivel (2021), decision-support value increases
when institutional records are converted into forecasting-capable signals. Hussain et al.
(2023) further demonstrates that trend modeling helps institutions prepare staffing and
resource allocations more effectively than static historical review alone.
