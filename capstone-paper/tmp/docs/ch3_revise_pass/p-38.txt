At the frontend layer, Inertia.js with React and TypeScript provides component-based
pages that map directly to role workflows. Shared UI patterns are used to keep task flow
predictable for high-frequency operations such as intake, posting, grading, and
verification.

The data design is relational and continuity-oriented: learner identity, enrollment state,
financial transactions, schedules, attendance, and grades are linked through validated
references so updates are reusable across modules without repeated encoding.

Process design is built around staged operational flow. Registrar intake initializes learner
context, finance posting updates balance state, enrichment validates identity continuity,
and academic modules consume authorized records for schedule and grading outputs.

Analytics design uses standardized KPI, alert, and trend payload contracts so dashboards
across roles can render consistent indicators while still reflecting role-specific context. This
allows school leadership and operational staff to interpret metrics from one coherent data
pipeline.

Governance design integrates authentication, permission visibility, audit traceability, and
configurable system controls. Together, these controls support accountability, policy
alignment, and secure institutional operation.

Overall, the product design treats MarriottConnect not as isolated feature pages but as
one coordinated school operations platform where transaction quality and decision
visibility are engineered as connected outcomes.
