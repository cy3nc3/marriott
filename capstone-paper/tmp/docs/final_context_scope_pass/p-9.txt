Academic scheduling and class-list coordination reveal a similar structural challenge.
Subject assignments, section constraints, and teacher availability must be reconciled in
ways that prevent conflicts while preserving instructional continuity. Manual cross-
checking can complete the task, but it often increases cycle time and revision overhead.
According to Muller et al. (2025), timetable quality improves when constraints are explicitly
modeled and resolved systematically, not manually improvised. In Marriott School's
context, this supports the move toward structured schedule governance where academic
planning outputs are reliable before dissemination to teachers, students, and parents.

Instructional workflows also require coherent links between attendance, grading, and
verification stages. If class-level records are maintained in disconnected channels,
teachers must repeatedly transfer and validate entries before submission, increasing the
chance of latency and inconsistency. According to Zulueta et al. (2021), socio-technical
alignment in MIS means process rules and system workflows must evolve together to
sustain reliability. MarriottConnect addresses this by treating attendance, score encoding,
grade submission, and verification checkpoints as connected institutional processes
rather than isolated encoding tasks.

Governance and access control requirements further shape the project context. Schools
need role boundaries that prevent unauthorized edits while still allowing authorized users
to access what they need on time. According to Kanona (2022), central database controls
and role-scoped access are key to protecting information integrity in academic systems.
The same governance logic is reflected in MarriottConnect through policy-driven access
controls, audit visibility, and announcement/notification traceability, so transparency is
achieved without sacrificing operational discipline.
