How can MarriottConnect be developed as an integrated student information system with
decision support analytics that centralizes registrar, finance, academic, and stakeholder
workflows to deliver timely, reliable, and role-governed decisions for Marriott School?

Marriott School's general problem is rooted in disconnected workflow architecture. The
Academic Head and office interviews indicate that departments still produce records in
separate channels and reconcile manually before output release, which extends cycle
time and reduces confidence in consolidated reports. According to Grepon et al. (2021),
integrated processing significantly improves operational continuity and reliability by
reducing file-level fragmentation and duplicate processing.

The same general pattern is reinforced by recent educational MIS research. According to
Kanona (2022), manual and fragmented record handling reduces both efficiency and data
integrity, while centralized database structures improve retrieval and control. Because
Marriott School requires synchronized registrar, finance, and academic decisions, the
general problem is not simply digitization; it is the need for one validated institutional data
flow that supports both daily transactions and leadership planning.
