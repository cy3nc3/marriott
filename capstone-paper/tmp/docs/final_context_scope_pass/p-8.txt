In enrollment periods, the registrar must process a large volume of learner entries under
time pressure while preserving LIS-oriented consistency. When intake records are
encoded in disconnected trackers, every downstream update introduces a new
opportunity for mismatch. According to Porlas et al. (2023), inconsistencies between LIS-
facing structures and school-level MIS records create recurring validation burden across
offices. For Marriott School, this means that what appears as a small intake encoding
delay can later affect payment confirmation, class-list finalization, and administrative
reporting confidence.

The record-continuity requirement extends beyond initial enrollment. Permanent records,
promotion decisions, and student-departure handling all depend on the same validated
learner identity baseline. If this baseline is fragmented, later academic and compliance
outputs become harder to verify and slower to release. According to Kanona (2022),
structured database-backed systems provide stronger retrieval reliability and control
integrity than manual, file-fragmented record handling. This supports MarriottConnect's
design principle that learner data should be encoded with continuity in mind rather than
re-encoded at every stage of institutional processing.

Finance workflows add another layer of operational sensitivity because billing accuracy
directly affects trust, cash-flow transparency, and release-dependent school transactions.
The finance interview evidence indicates that balance confirmation often depends on
manual sequence checks when multiple payments occur in close succession. According
to Wulandari and Pinandito (2021), tuition-related decision support improves when
payment-status logic is formalized and system-driven. Chai and Mostafa (2021) also
report that integrated student-finance data handling reduces duplicate effort while
improving cross-role visibility, which is critical for parent communication and internal
reconciliation timing.
