At the leadership level, fragmented records also weaken decision support. Historical data
may exist, but when data pipelines are disconnected, trend interpretation becomes late
and less actionable. According to Esquivel and Esquivel (2021), enrollment-related
decision support improves when institutional records are transformed into forecasting
inputs rather than static archives. Hussain et al. (2023) further show that comparative
forecasting approaches can provide stronger planning indicators for staffing, sectioning,
and resource allocation.

For these reasons, the project context is best understood as an operational integration
challenge: multiple offices depend on the same student lifecycle data but require different
role-scoped actions. MarriottConnect responds by enforcing one validated data lifecycle
from intake, posting, enrichment, and academic processing up to dashboard-ready
analytics and governance outputs. In this architecture, data consistency, access control,
and decision visibility are treated as interdependent design requirements.

synchronization, academic reliability, and leadership-ready decision support.

A deeper view of the present environment shows that data fragmentation is not limited to
one office transaction. It propagates across the entire student lifecycle, from enrollment
intake and payment posting to section assignment, attendance recording, and report
generation. According to Zulueta et al. (2021), MIS environments in educational
institutions fail when workflow standardization is weak, even when digital tools are
available. This finding mirrors Marriott School's operational reality where records may exist
in digital format but still require repeated reconciliation before they can be trusted for
release, compliance, or planning.
