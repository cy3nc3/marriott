Wulandari and Pinandito (2021), tuition-related decision support improves when payment-

Billing transparency is not only an accounting concern; it is a stakeholder-service concern.
Families rely on timely balance updates, payment history visibility, and consistent
communication channels, especially during high-demand periods. When billing records are
delayed, schools face repeated follow-ups that consume office time and weaken confidence
in published statements. According to Navarra and Antonio (2025), monitoring platforms
improve communication outcomes when data is role-scoped, timely, and accessible
through one connected environment. This aligns with MarriottConnect's integrated parent
and finance views where institutional records and stakeholder-facing status updates remain
synchronized.

Academic scheduling and class-list coordination reveal a similar structural challenge.
Subject assignments, section constraints, and teacher availability must be reconciled in
ways that prevent conflicts while preserving instructional continuity. Manual cross-
checking can complete the task, but it often increases cycle time and revision overhead.
According to Muller et al. (2025), timetable quality improves when constraints are
explicitly modeled and resolved systematically, not manually improvised. In Marriott
School's context, this supports the move toward structured schedule governance where
academic planning outputs are reliable before dissemination to teachers, students, and
parents.

Instructional workflows also require coherent links between attendance, grading, and
verification stages. If class-level records are maintained in disconnected channels, teachers
must repeatedly transfer and validate entries before submission, increasing the chance of
latency and inconsistency. According to Zulueta et al. (2021), socio-technical alignment in
MIS means process rules and system workflows must evolve together to sustain reliability.
