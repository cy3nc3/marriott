Academic coordination is likewise affected. The registrar and teacher interviews indicate
that schedule preparation, class-list alignment, and grade validation involve repetitive
verification cycles before release. According to Muller et al. (2025), timetable quality and
conflict reduction depend on explicit constraint handling rather than manual plotting.
Zulueta et al. (2021) also show that process standardization and workflow discipline are
critical in educational MIS environments, reinforcing the need for structured academic
operations across sections, subjects, and grade submissions.

Stakeholder communication pressure further amplifies the issue. Parent-facing updates on
grades, schedules, and billing are less effective when underlying records are delayed or
inconsistently synchronized across offices. According to Navarra and Antonio (2025),
web-based monitoring systems improve transparency and usability when authorized users
receive timely, role-scoped visibility from one connected record environment. This aligns
with Marriott School's need to reduce ad hoc follow-ups and provide dependable status
communication to families.

At the leadership level, fragmented records also weaken decision support. Historical data
may exist, but when data pipelines are disconnected, trend interpretation becomes late and
less actionable. According to Esquivel and Esquivel (2021), enrollment-related decision
support improves when institutional records are transformed into forecasting inputs rather
than static archives. Hussain et al. (2023) further show that comparative forecasting
approaches can provide stronger planning indicators for staffing, sectioning, and resource
allocation.

For these reasons, the project context is best understood as an operational integration
challenge: multiple offices depend on the same student lifecycle data but require different
role-scoped actions. MarriottConnect responds by enforcing one validated data lifecycle
from intake, posting, enrichment, and academic processing up to dashboard-ready analytics
and governance outputs. In this architecture, data consistency, access control, and decision
visibility are treated as interdependent design requirements.
