The model includes the following stages, ensuring a controlled and user-centered
development process:

    1. Requirements Gathering and Analysis

        Interviews, workflow mapping, and role-task validation are conducted to identify
        bottlenecks in enrollment, finance posting, scheduling, and grading.

    2. Quick Design

        Initial page flow, data mapping, and module interaction designs are prepared based
        on validated role responsibilities and system boundaries.

    3. Build Prototype

        A working prototype is implemented for core modules including registrar intake,
        cashier posting, teacher grading workflows, and role dashboards.

    4. Initial User Evaluation

        Key school stakeholders evaluate behavior, usability, and data consistency to
        confirm whether outputs match real operational needs.

    5. Refining Prototype

        Feedback is applied iteratively to improve accuracy, workflow speed, and interface
        clarity until the solution satisfies acceptance conditions.

    6. Implement and Maintain

        Once validated, the solution proceeds to controlled implementation, user
        orientation, and maintenance planning for sustained operational use.
