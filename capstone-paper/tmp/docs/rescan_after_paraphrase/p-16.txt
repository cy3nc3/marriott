    ➢ To develop role dashboards and governance controls that produce actionable
        indicators for enrollment trends, payment behavior, and operational risk
        monitoring.

        This objective is relevant because leadership planning requires decision-ready
        indicators derived from validated operational data. Based on the research by
        Esquivel and Esquivel (2021), utilizing historical data in decision-making
        processes can enhance the value of decision-support systems. Hussain et al. (2023)
        further demonstrates how trend forecasting can aid in planning for resources and
        staffing, both of which contributes to a justification for developing KPI dashboards,
        governance controls, and analytics as primary functions within institutions.


Scope


The proposed system provides module-based access for seven core roles: super admin,
admin, registrar, finance, teacher, student, and parent. Access is structured by institutional
responsibility and each module groups related actions into one operational unit.

The scope centralizes enrollment, finance, academic, governance, and stakeholder
communication workflows while assigning each role to complete modules that include
transaction handling, validation, and analytics interpretation.

The Registrar can access the following:

    ● Enrollment Module - Manage intake creation, profile updates, and payment-linked
        progression within one registrar workflow.
    ● Student Directory Module - Maintain searchable learner profiles, identity
        continuity, and authorized record retrieval.
    ● SF1 Import Module - Upload SF1 records and execute LRN-based enrichment
        aligned with LIS-oriented validation rules.
    ● Permanent Records Module - Maintain historical academic records and document
        continuity across school years.
    ● Batch Promotion and Student Departure Module - Process promotion outcomes
