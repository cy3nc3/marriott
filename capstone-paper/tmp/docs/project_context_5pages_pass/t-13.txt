Specific Objectives:

➢ To develop a centralized registrar workflow that captures enrollment intake,
supports SF1 upload, and enriches learner records by LRN to reduce duplicate
encoding.
This objective directly responds to registrar-side duplication and validation delays
observed in the appendices. According to Grepon et al. (2021), unified admissions
and records pipelines reduce processing time and increase data reliability. Porlas
et al. (2023) further supports LIS-aware alignment, making this objective essential
for preserving data continuity from intake through reporting.

➢ To develop integrated cashiering and billing workflows that automate posting,
update balances immediately, and keep ledgers and parent billing views
synchronized.
This objective is relevant because finance operations require immediate,
trustworthy balance visibility to support both internal reconciliation and parent
communication. According to Wulandari and Pinandito (2021), structured financial
decision logic improves consistency in tuition management. Chai and Mostafa
(2021) also supports integrated financial-record handling as a practical mechanism
for reducing cross-check burden and improving transparency.

➢ To develop schedule and class-list workflows that support academic planning while
minimizing timetable conflicts and repetitive manual verification.
This objective addresses recurring scheduling friction in section and teacher
assignment workflows. According to Muller et al. (2025), conflict-free scheduling
quality improves when constraints are explicitly modeled and resolved
systematically. Zulueta et al. (2021) reinforces that standardized workflows are
necessary for sustainable MIS operations in educational institutions.

➢ To develop secure student and parent portals that provide authorized visibility to
schedules, grades, and billing information.
